What is Pharming?

Pharming is a type of cyber attack where attackers manipulate DNS settings or use malicious software to redirect users to fake websites, often for the purpose of stealing sensitive information.

How Pharming Works:

Attackers can alter DNS records or use malware to modify a user's hosts file, causing their computer to connect to a fraudulent website instead of the legitimate one.

Simulating a Successful Pharming Attempt:

To simulate a successful pharming attempt, enter a URL that corresponds to a fraudulent website. This would be a website that attackers are attempting to impersonate.
for this project just type "fraudulent-website.com", any other input defaults to failure.

Simulating a Failed Pharming Attempt:

To simulate a failed pharming attempt, enter a URL that is legitimate and not associated with a phishing or fraudulent website.

Preventing Pharming Attacks:

  • Regularly update your computer's operating system and software.
  • Use trusted and up-to-date antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Be cautious when clicking on links in emails or messages, especially if they look suspicious.
  • Verify the URL of websites, especially when entering sensitive information.
  • Use a reputable DNS service provider.
