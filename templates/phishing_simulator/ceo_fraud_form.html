What is CEO Fraud (Business Email Compromise)?

CEO Fraud, also known as Business Email Compromise (BEC), is a type of cyber attack where attackers impersonate company executives or leaders to trick employees into taking unauthorized actions, such as transferring funds or sharing sensitive information.

How CEO Fraud Works:

Attackers use social engineering techniques to craft convincing emails that appear to come from a high-ranking executive within the company. These emails often contain urgent requests for financial transactions or sensitive data.

Simulating a Successful CEO Fraud Attempt:

To simulate a successful CEO Fraud attempt, enter an email message that mimics a convincing request from a company executive. Make it appear urgent and persuasive.
for this project just type "CEO's urgent request", any other input defaults to failure.

Simulating a Failed CEO Fraud Attempt:

To simulate a failed CEO Fraud attempt, enter an email message that is not convincing or does not resemble an urgent request from a company executive.

Preventing CEO Fraud (BEC) Attacks:

  • Always verify the authenticity of email requests for financial transactions or sensitive data.
  • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for financial transactions.
  • Educate employees about CEO Fraud and provide training to recognize suspicious emails.
  • Have clear policies and procedures for verifying and authorizing financial transactions.
  • Use email security solutions to detect and filter out phishing emails.
